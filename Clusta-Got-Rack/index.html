Seriously though, focus on your use cases and the value data can add to your products. If you legitimately have terabytes to petabytes of data, use Hadoop or other appropriate infastructure to handle this volume of data. However, most use cases don't require this. A creative analyst using a laptop can generate brilliant results without all the extra hassle the majority of the time. As Microsoft wrote, Nobody ever got fired for using Hadoop on a cluster, but most times it was a waste.
